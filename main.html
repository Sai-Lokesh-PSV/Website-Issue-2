Editor's Note

We are proud to present the second issue of the FKS Tribune magazine! I hope you all are excited to divulge this issue’s topic: Human Relations. (Trust me it’s not as boring as it sounds). The Topic of Human Relations regards the way humans have interacted for centuries as well as the modern age of 2021. Thoughts, opinions and criticisms are all man-made notions, relating to Human Relations and fundamental to our daily life, be it professionally, socially or personally.
... Why?
In this issue, we will be discussing just that, with a variety of global happenings and recent discoveries, cemented together by human nature and psychology. Here are some of the articles we hope you love! Our Environment section will be sending you into deep thought regarding the state of the earth with its articles about the ghostly corals around the world and the deathly pollution taking over our world. In our Cultural section, we have the science behind music therapy, Literary; exploring the diversity of poetry and Art; our art gallery and a glimpse of techniques used to paint for centuries. Wonder, a world-renowned book, focusing on the underlying cruel and discriminating society we live in, through the experiences of a facially deformed child; Is the selected book for our Book Review this issue. We all know it’s Indian Premier League season but we promise you reading our Sports section will surprise you with all sorts of facts about your favourite players and their lives in digestible segments. The Featured Column brings attention to some of the best nonprofits collaborating with The Future Kid’s School or run by our very own students, including ventures such as A Teenager’s Interest, with whom many of you are familiar with and a recent blog set up by our very own seniors; Ishita, Sanjana, Aryan and Aaditi from grade 12. Coming to our FKS News, we have first-hand reports of events hosted by FKS and their results school-wide, giving you updates of our school’s experiences! Our FKS MUN and inter-house competitions are notable events for all grades. Our Current Affairs section is just what you need during this tense period with rumours of a nationwide lockdown to fake information that vaccines cause terminal illnesses. We will be delving into the COVID scares in the Indian Premier League, China’s Space Dream and more! We have a very special article on this issue, the first of its kind in FKS history. Thought-provoking article, discussing the concept of history’s value in the future and its place in our lives. A gem for you mystery lovers and sure to catch anyone’s attention is our Unknown Stories. Photographs are the key concept we focus on here, for example, Albert Einstein’s very famous photograph (used largely for memes) and Gandhiji's spinning wheel photograph. We come yet again to our Fun Facts column giving you a blissful read of brain teaser, riddles and crosswords to keep your mind sharp and strong. A challenge we just know you’ll enjoy! Last but not least is our General Knowledge section displaying topics and concepts you might not have heard about otherwise. Hyper-loop to Petcare through the issues you will find it all.
Like always, we put blood, sweat and tears into this issue and The Tribune’s primary motive is to give you a deeper understanding of the world you live in and help you form well thought out, informed opinions. I hope this issue, as well as those to come, does just that! Happy Reading!!

-Kiran Mehta

Year of New Reality

The year 2020 started off like any other year. Everyone was looking forward to the new academic year, new friends, new resolutions, goals and aspirations. 2020 surprisingly had something unexpected planned for all of us.

FKS was off to a great start at the beginning of the year. All the students were involved in different activities and courses of the inter house competition between the Whiz and the Super house.

In India, during the first week of February the COVID19 cases were gradually increasing. The schools started shutting down, and by the second week of March ‘Lockdown’ was called by the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi. A preventive measure to contain the spread of COVID-19.

As the lockdown was during March it was not far from the summer vacation which is usually in the first week of April. So most of the schools have announced summer break for all the grades one to eight. Mercifully, the time favored where all the grades had finished their final term assessments.


Image courtesy :

Image courtesy :

There were some immediate measures to shift the education. The education system scrambled to find alternative options to resume schooling. Our school introduced online classes on ‘Vedamo’ for grades nine and above to experiment the new way of learning.

Students, teachers, parents and everyone were curious to analyze the challenges and benefits of revolutionary change in schooling. Stable high speed internet access suddenly gained importance in the midst of this pandemic. Many teachers were not equipped with appropriate devices for delivering smooth classes.

Most of the students and teachers got all their internet/WI-FI cables fixed and use of technical knowledge was needed. The priority moved to learn software's tools like zoom PowerPoint presentation

The drastic transition to an ‘online class room’ was not easy to adapt for students also. Children were missing the energetic atmosphere of the classroom. The shouting, mingling, teasing and running around was being missed out and was restricted only to an online chat. This was affecting the children's physical and mental growth.

White board markers to a white screen and writing pages to typing keys.

Online platform ‘Vedamo’ was replaced by ‘CodeTantra’ which is still being used for classes. Additionally we have zoom rooms for each grade. CodeTantra was being used for all the academics while Zoom was being used for assembly, group activities and teacher’s meetings.

Image courtesy :
https://qz.com/work/1929629/how-to-prepare-for-a-future-where-creativity-is-a-survival-skill/

Kids got opportunities to think outside the box when they let go off the negativity and aimed their efforts towards creativity.

Our schedules for each day had a period called ‘Offline and Chatting’, The offline, where all the students get a hard copy of a worksheet sent by the school for each month (when the cases were low and safe). This was provided so that we students do not strain our eyes sitting in front of our screens which gave us sufficient time to give a break from the device.

The chatting time is where all the grades have fun and interaction with our fellow peers and teachers, this interaction with the teacher and students was a mellow stress buster free time.

Grade 9 (Cabinet of 2020 - 2021) The grade nine students were an extraordinary, unique batch of students who have shown great commitment during every event and celebrations of the year. It was the year which taught us how to balance our studies as well as fun, during the planning time for the events. Having a must experience to work alongside the FKS family opened our eyes to the outside world. It gave us an experience which builds the foundation for our near future. The school has always believed in giving a fair and equal opportunity to develop our character traits. We have always been and will be prepared for the ‘future’ and bring a value to our opinions and perspective.

Image courtesy :
https://www.digitalhrtech.com/interview-guide/

Our interviews were not like any other year. Being online the seniors who conduct each stage of interviews for every student of grade nine has been an experience which has helped us look at ourselves in different angles.

Personally by the time I have gone through all the rounds of the interviews with different questions and different seniors and teachers with different purpose of teaching which gives us a learning experience that life outside will treat you very hard and when you aim to go high, you should remember to stay grounded because when you fall you won’t hurt yourself hard. Keeping your eyes on the stars and the feet on the ground has helped us stay calm and Courageous.

By the time I have completed going through all the rounds of the cabinet interview process, I definitely have grown as an individual and built a strong foundation for myself.

Image courtesy : https://thefederal.com/features/political-campaigns-must-be-eco-friendly-sustainable/

My campaigning experience was incredible, where I used my creativity alongside my close friends who have supported me throughout the campaign. I had got my symbol, slogan, posters and great friends to support me during the campaign. There was fun and excitement in the air although we were not at school I felt the positive vibes!

Planning for the events was a good spirit of hard work and team work. It is when our grade comes together and pitches in different ideas for different events. Our batch 2020 - 2021 was the first batch to have our cabinet ‘online’.

We are proud to say that we are the batch of the ‘online year’ because we had a completely different approach to different problems and have valued our bond and worked as a team with great sportsman spirit and have accepted all the differences, challenges and obstacles in our way. Although we were not at school we still had the spirit and excitement to work towards something big. All of us in the past, as juniors have always looked forward to this ‘cabinet year’. Unfortunately we did not get to do the events at school but at the end of the day we have learnt to take everyone's strengths and fight the challenges and organized ourselves over the internet.

The FKS family has made exceptional efforts to make the students feel just like they would, physically in school. The teachers have also learnt from the tech savvy kids and have made learning fun, taking the best use of what everyone has.

Image courtesy : https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/soft-idli-recipe-using-idli-rava/

During the month of January 2021 schools were announced open from first of February 2021, the month our school’s birthday falls on. Our grade got very excited hearing this news! We had finished our assessment and immediately planned for our first in person school event. We were very lucky to have a chance to plan an event and get the experience to work at school and also see everyone after a very long time!

The school endeavoured to send wonderful, unforgettable gifts for Christmas, when the COVID 19 cases were controlled and everyone was following the safety precautions. The gift was the school's tasty, yummy ‘Sambar and Idli’. School ensured that each and everyone had received the gift and had got the joy of tasting the school's food after a long time!

All grades have gotten used to the online atmosphere and still have a feeling of getting back to school. Many students have the friendly and family like bond which is attached and developed to school over the past years and call it their ‘Second Home’. They really missed the physical school environment and the lovely food at school.

The FKS family has made exceptional efforts to make the students feel just like they would, physically in school. The teachers have also learnt from the tech savvy kids and have made learning fun, taking the best use of what everyone has.

We at FKS believe Dare to dream and lead by example’‘. Our teacher’s, didi’s and bhaiya’s have made efforts to be the best ‘example who have led by example and inspired the FKS family’ right from the beginning of the online year and supported everyone during hard times faced, making each day filled with joy and laughter.

The students stood for ‘dare to dream’, to take action, initiation, and make the best use of an online platform.

This year , the online platform helped establish a basic foundation in the passage of time. Teachers looked at teaching from various perspectives of each student’s view and helped us students remotely build our character stronger and stay grounded.

-Kaushal Talluri Deputy Head Boy
Grade X
