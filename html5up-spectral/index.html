Why should one paint?
Painting sharpens the mind through conceptual visualization and implementation, plus, boosts memory skills. People using creative outlets such as writing, painting and drawing have less chance of developing memory loss illnesses when they get older. It is not compulsory to learn but helps you be creative. It helps one to visualize and communicate with one another. Painting provides a relaxing, open environment where artists feel safe to explore their creativity and encourages a more optimistic approach to life. It helps you express one's emotions and thoughts that are part of eternal human nature and has a universal appeal. Acrylics are more popular nowadays as it is easier to use, more cost-effective, and versatile. So in this article, we will be focusing on acrylics.

HISTORY
Otto Röhm invented acrylic resin- [An Acrylic resin is a thermoplastic substance typically derived from acrylic acid] which was quickly transformed into acrylic paint. Synthetic paint was first used in the 1940s, combining some of the properties of oil and watercolour. mineral spirit-based paints were introduced between 1946 and 1949 (professional paints that dry quickly). Acrylics were made commercially available in the 1950s.

What is Acrylic Paint?
Water-based acrylic paint is composed of pigment particles dispersed in an acrylic polymer emulsion. There are three main components in any acrylic paint - pigment, binder and vehicle:
1)Pigment - pigments are granular solids that give the paint its colour. They are milled to tiny particle size and do not dissolve, but remain suspended in the paint.

2)Binder - a binder is the substance that keeps pigment in place after the paint dries. Acrylic paint has acrylic polymer as its binder.

3)Vehicle - this refers to the part of the paint that carries the pigment and binder. Water is the vehicle for water-based acrylic paints. Once the water leaves the system via evaporation, the paint dries, creating a stable polymer film full of trapped coloured pigment particles.

Acrylic colours dry as a result of water evaporation. Here’s what occurs as pigment, water and acrylic are transformed into a long-lasting paint film. Acrylic paint is a finely balanced dispersion of pigment in acrylic polymer and water. The water serves as the emulsion liquid and acts as a kind of chemical ‘chaperone’. When exposed to the atmosphere, water in the emulsion evaporates or is absorbed into the painting support. That’s when the acrylic polymer particles come into direct contact and fuse with each other. The clear polymer particles organize themselves into a stable, hexagonal structure, trapping the pigment in place and creating the paint film which is extremely stable, water-resistant, permanent and vibrant.

Methods

Dry Brush


Uses a brush that has not been dipped in water. Creates scratchy textured strokes

Washing

Involves diluting the brush with water to create a translucent paint, quite similar to watercolours.

Stippling

Usage of the brush to create an assemblage of tiny dots, often used in landscape painting.

Splattering

Flicking of very wet brush dipped in paint to give an unpredictable or abstract effect

Dabbing

Use of cloth or stiff brush to apply paint with light pressure adds movements or illustrates greenery.

Sponging

Use of cellulose sponge to dab to create an irregular textured pattern.

Palette knife


Applying paint using the edge of palette knife, similar to frosting a cake, creating sweeping flat layers.

Detailing

Using a fine round brush to finish the minute details when nearing completion of the artwork.

Glazing

Mixing a thin translucent film called glaze with acrylic paint to create a rich luminous hue. By adding more layers, a stained glass effect is created.

Why Are acrylics preferred over oil paints?

Acrylics have water as the vehicle whereas oils use either turpentine or linseed oil as the vehicle. Oils give a glossy finish and last very long, which makes it preferable to professional artists. On the other hand, acrylics give a basic finish and are peeled off quickly due to their thin coat and are not very durable. Acrylics dry very quickly so are harder to work with whereas oils are time-taking but have a good finish. Although oils are very expensive to use and need constant replenishing and acrylics are way cheaper.
